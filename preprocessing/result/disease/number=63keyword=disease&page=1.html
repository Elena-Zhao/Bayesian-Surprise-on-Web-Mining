autoimmune related diseases association), the term autoimmune disease refers to a varied group of more than 80 serious, chronic illnesses that involve almost every human organ system. in all of these diseases, the underlying problem is similar, the body's immune system becomes misdirected, attacking the very organs it was designed to protect. research of autoimmune diseases has not revealed the mechanisms that cause this response. according to a.d.a.m, one theory holds that various microorganisms and drugs somehow trigger the misdirected immune response, particularly in those with a genetic predisposition to an autoimmune disease. it is possible to have more than one autoimmune disease. many types of arthritis are considered autoimmune-related diseases, including: ankylosing spondylitis
